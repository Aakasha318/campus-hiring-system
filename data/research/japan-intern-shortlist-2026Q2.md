## University briefs

### 1. The University of Tokyo (東京大学 / Tōdai)

- **City:** Tokyo (Hongō / Komaba campuses)
- **Recognition:** THE World 2025 #26; QS World 2024 #32 (both #1 in Japan). Member of **RU11**. MEXT **Top Global University Project — Type A (Top Type)**.
- **Relevant programs:** Faculty of Engineering (one of Japan's largest); Graduate School of Information Science and Technology; Graduate School of Engineering. THE subject ranks: Engineering #29, CS #33 globally. International students ~17.7% of student body; postgraduate international share ~31% (Nov 2023).
- **Career center / placement office:** **Career Support Office** (キャリアサポート室), within UTokyo's central student services; faculty-level career support also exists per department. URL: https://www.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/prospective-students/career_support.html. Named POC: not publicly listed.
- **Industry internship support:** UTokyo runs **UTRIP** (UTokyo Research Internship Program — research-oriented, not industry SDE). The Career Support Office promotes employer seminars and job-hunting events but a flagship long-term industry internship program is not publicly highlighted. Most engineering placements follow lab-mediated channels.
- **English-medium engineering:** Partial. **PEAK** (Programs in English at Komaba) is liberal-arts/environmental sciences focused — not engineering-heavy. **GSC** (Graduate Programs in English) covers many engineering/IST tracks at the Master's/PhD level. Undergraduate engineering is mostly Japanese.
- **International student / non-Japanese-speaking support:** International Student Support Room; multiple English-speaking advisors; Guide for International Students.
- **Prior hiring by non-Japanese tech firms:** UTokyo graduates are commonly hired by Google Japan, IBM Japan, Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, Accenture Japan (widely reported in Japanese press and alumni bios; no single placement statistic publicly listed by the university).
- **Fit score:** **7 / 10**
- **Rationale:**
  - Strongest CS/engineering brand in Japan; high-quality candidates.
  - English-medium pipeline is graduate-skewed, which actually suits Celebal's SDE intern profile.
  - Career office is decentralized and selective — outreach friction is real but doable.
- **Risks / notes:** Heavy competition from gaishi-kei FAANG-tier firms; shinsotsu (new-graduate mass-hiring) cycle dominates senior year; UTokyo students may view a 6-month internship as competing with research. Best target: Master's students in IST/Engineering. [NEEDS REFRESH: confirm post-2024 career office org chart.]

### 2. Institute of Science Tokyo / Science Tokyo (科学大 — formerly 東京工業大学 Tokyo Tech)

- **City:** Tokyo (Ōokayama / Suzukakedai / Yushima campuses)
- **Recognition:** Formed **1 October 2024** by merger of Tokyo Institute of Technology + Tokyo Medical and Dental University. Inherits Tokyo Tech's QS World ~#85 / THE ~#166 positions [NEEDS REFRESH: 2024]. Member of **RU11**. MEXT **Top Global University Project — Type A** (both predecessor institutions held Type A status).
- **Relevant programs:** Six schools including **School of Engineering** and **School of Computing** (CS, Information & Communications Engineering, Mathematical & Computing Science). ~13,358 students (2023); strongly STEM-focused.
- **Career center / placement office:** Career-support function inherited from Tokyo Tech ("Career Office" / キャリアアドバイザー); post-merger integration ongoing. Specific Science Tokyo URL not yet stable. Named POC: not publicly listed.
- **Industry internship support:** Tokyo Tech historically operated structured industry internships (summer/long-term) tied to lab-industry collaborations. Cooperative education with Japanese OEMs (Toyota, Hitachi, Sony) is established. Whether long-term (6-month) off-cycle internships for foreign firms are actively supported is not publicly confirmed.
- **English-medium engineering:** **Yes (graduate-strong)**. The **International Graduate Program in Engineering and Sciences (IGES/IGP)** offers full English-medium Master's and PhD across engineering and computing. Undergraduate is predominantly Japanese with selected English courses.
- **International student / non-Japanese-speaking support:** Strong — multiple overseas bases (Thailand, Germany, US, Philippines, China, Egypt, Ghana, Chile), International Student Exchange Division.
- **Prior hiring by non-Japanese tech firms:** Tokyo Tech alumni are heavily represented at Google Japan, Amazon Japan, Microsoft Japan, Indeed, Mercari (often cited in Japanese tech press). No university-published statistics.
- **Fit score:** **9 / 10**
- **Rationale:**
  - Highest CS/engineering concentration of any Japanese university; best technical baseline.
  - IGES graduate cohort is English-fluent, international, and accustomed to global firms.
  - Lab-industry collaboration culture lowers friction for structured long-term internships.
- **Risks / notes:** Post-merger administrative changes may cause POC churn through 2025–2026; verify which entity owns career operations. [NEEDS REFRESH: post-merger 2024 org structure.]

### 3. Waseda University (早稲田大学)

- **City:** Tokyo (Waseda / Nishi-Waseda / Toyama campuses)
- **Recognition:** QS World 2023 #196; THE World 601-800 band [NEEDS REFRESH: 2023]. Private university. Member of **RU11**. MEXT **Top Global University Project — Type A**.
- **Relevant programs:** Three engineering schools — **Fundamental Science and Engineering (FSE)**, **Creative Science and Engineering (CSE)**, **Advanced Science and Engineering (ASE)** — with corresponding graduate schools. Computer Science & Communications Engineering, Computer Science & Engineering departments. Significant undergrad scale.
- **Career center / placement office:** **Waseda Career Center** (早稲田大学キャリアセンター). URL: https://www.waseda.jp/inst/career/en/. Tel: 03-3203-4332; email: career@list.waseda.jp. Operates dedicated international-student career support track.
- **Industry internship support:** **Yes — active**. Waseda Career Center publicly promotes summer internships, industry research fairs, and an explicit "international student practical seminar" track. Internship programs are integrated into career-center calendar. Long-term (6-month) industry internships less standard but feasible via lab/department channels.
- **English-medium engineering:** Partial. **English-based Degree Programs** exist in FSE/CSE/ASE (e.g., Computer Science & Communications Engineering English track; Mechanical Engineering English track). Plus the **School of International Liberal Studies (SILS)** for non-engineering.
- **International student / non-Japanese-speaking support:** Strong — Waseda has the largest international student body of any Japanese university (~8,000+ historically); robust English support; visa-recommendation-letter service.
- **Prior hiring by non-Japanese tech firms:** Waseda is one of the most-recruited campuses in Japan by gaishi-kei firms — IBM Japan, Accenture, Deloitte, Goldman Sachs, Amazon Japan, and Indian IT firms (TCS Japan, Infosys Japan) have visible alumni presence (no university-published placement stats).
- **Fit score:** **9 / 10**
- **Rationale:**
  - Most internationally oriented top-tier private; explicit international-student career track.
  - Career Center is centralized, contactable, and used to engaging foreign employers.
  - English-track engineering students are an exact fit for Celebal's SDE intern profile.
- **Risks / notes:** Highly competitive — Waseda students are heavily courted by gaishi-kei; Celebal must offer differentiated value. Long-term off-cycle 6-month commitment may clash with shinsotsu prep in 4th year.

### 4. Keio University (慶應義塾大学)

- **City:** Tokyo / Yokohama (Mita, Hiyoshi, Yagami, Shonan Fujisawa Campus/SFC)
- **Recognition:** QS World ~#215; THE World 601-800 band [NEEDS REFRESH: 2023-24]. Private university. Member of **RU11**. MEXT **Top Global University Project — Type A**.
- **Relevant programs:** **Faculty of Science and Technology** (Yagami campus, ~932 first-year intake) — Departments include Information & Computer Science, Applied Physics & Physico-Informatics, Mathematics, etc. **Faculty of Environment and Information Studies (SFC)** and **Faculty of Policy Management (SFC)** are interdisciplinary with strong CS/data/IT exposure.
- **Career center / placement office:** **Office for Students (Career Services)** / 学生部就職・進路支援担当. URL: https://www.keio.ac.jp/en/student-life/career-support/ (note: specific endpoint may have changed — verify). Named POC: not publicly listed.
- **Industry internship support:** Keio promotes internships through both central career services and faculty-level channels. SFC has a particularly strong project/entrepreneurship culture with industry collaborations. Long-term (6-month) internships are more feasible at SFC than at traditional faculties.
- **English-medium engineering:** Partial. **GIGA Program** at SFC (Global Information and Governance Academic Program) is an English-medium bachelor's covering policy/environment/information — touches CS but is not a pure engineering track. **PEARL** (Economics) and other English programs exist but engineering proper is mostly Japanese. Graduate Science & Tech has English-track options.
- **International student / non-Japanese-speaking support:** Solid; smaller international population than Waseda but well-resourced.
- **Prior hiring by non-Japanese tech firms:** Keio is a top recruiting target for global consulting (McKinsey, BCG, Accenture), investment banking, and tech firms (Google Japan, Amazon Japan). Keio SFC alumni are heavily represented in Japan's startup ecosystem (Mercari, SmartNews, etc.).
- **Fit score:** **7 / 10**
- **Rationale:**
  - SFC's culture explicitly fits long-term projects/internships — a clear bullseye.
  - Strong brand; alumni network supportive.
  - But Faculty of Science & Tech is more traditionally Japanese-medium and shinsotsu-oriented.
- **Risks / notes:** Keio Faculty of S&T students are heavily courted by domestic OEMs and gaishi-kei finance; differentiate via SDE technical depth. SFC is the right entry point.

## Synthesis

### Ranked shortlist for Celebal's program

1. **Institute of Science Tokyo (Science Tokyo)** — Highest CS/engineering concentration in Japan; IGES English-medium grad programs are a precise fit; lab-industry culture lowers internship friction.
2. **Nagoya University** — Unique G30 English-medium *undergraduate* engineering pipeline; RU11 quality; matches Nagoya geo target directly.
3. **Waseda University** — Most international-friendly career center in Japan; explicit international-student career track; English engineering degree options across FSE/CSE/ASE.
4. **The University of Tokyo** — Top brand; English-medium graduate engineering (GSC); but career office decentralized and candidates are highly courted.
5. **Osaka University** — Top Kansai engineering brand; English Master's tracks (though Mechanical English-program contraction is a warning sign); matches Osaka geo target.
6. **Keio University** — Strongest at SFC for interdisciplinary CS/data projects; Faculty of S&T more traditional. GIGA program useful but not pure SDE.
7. **Nagoya Institute of Technology** — Smaller scale, lower brand-competition, IGPGE pipeline; good "secondary feeder" for Nagoya.
8. **Osaka Metropolitan University** — Newest institution; smallest English-program footprint; useful for volume but lowest English-readiness signal.

### Recommended first-touch (top 3 to approach first)

1. **Institute of Science Tokyo** — Approach the **IGES / International Graduate Program** coordinator and the School of Computing's international office. Highest density of English-capable, technically deep SDE candidates in the entire shortlist; lab-mediated long-term internships are culturally accepted.
2. **Nagoya University G30 Office** — The only university on the list with English-medium undergraduate engineering at scale; engaging the G30 coordinator and Graduate School of Informatics simultaneously gives access to both undergraduate interns and Master's-level candidates in Celebal's Nagoya target city.
3. **Waseda Career Center (International Student Career Support track)** — Phone 03-3203-4332, email career@list.waseda.jp. The most contactable, internationalized, gaishi-kei-experienced career office among the eight; precedent for foreign-firm engagement is strongest.

### Critical risks / things to validate before outreach

- **Shinsotsu (新卒一括採用) cycle dominance**: Japanese 4th-year undergraduates and 2nd-year Master's students are funneled into April mass-hiring; off-cycle 6-month internships compete directly with job-hunting (shūkatsu / 就活). Celebal should target **1st-year Master's**, **3rd-year G30/PEAK/IGES undergrads**, and **PhD students** rather than terminal-year students.
- **Conversion-to-FTE expectations**: Japanese students value job security; if Celebal's intern-to-FTE conversion involves relocation to India, conversion rates will be low. A Japan-based FTE pathway (or hybrid) is near-mandatory for serious offers.
- **Visa support**: Most foreign students hold "Student" status of residence; converting to "Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services" requires sponsor cooperation. Confirm Celebal's Japan-entity sponsorship capacity before promising visa support to candidates.
- **Career-office formality**: Japanese career offices generally require formal written introduction (a 依頼状 / *irai-jō* — request letter on company letterhead) before engaging with foreign employers. Cold outreach via web forms is typically ignored.
- **Post-merger administrative churn**: Science Tokyo (2024) and OMU (2022) are still integrating — POCs and URLs change. Verify any contact before publishing it internally at Celebal.
- **Compensation benchmarks**: Stipends for engineering interns at Japanese universities are typically ¥150,000–300,000/month for long-term industry roles; gaishi-kei firms often pay higher. Celebal must benchmark stipend competitively or candidates will choose Google Japan/Indeed/Mercari instead.
- **Language reality**: "English a plus" framing should be **"English required, Japanese a plus"** in messaging, otherwise Celebal will receive applications from candidates whose English is too weak to function in an Indian-led delivery team. Pre-screen with a structured English interview.

### Pragmatic next steps

1. **Send formal introduction packets** (Japanese + English, company letterhead, MD signature) to the career offices of the top-3 (Science Tokyo IGES, Nagoya G30, Waseda Career Center). Target window: September–November (Japanese internship recruitment kickoff for the following year).
2. **Engage MEXT-affiliated channels**: Apply to the **Career Forum / Boston Career Forum (Tokyo Summer/Winter editions)** organized by DISCO Inc. — the single largest Japan-based event matching English-capable students with foreign-affiliated firms. Also evaluate the **JOB Hunting in Japan for International Students** events (JASSO).
3. **Partner with a Japan-based recruiting agency** (e.g., Robert Walters Japan, Daijob, GaijinPot) for the first 1–2 cycles to validate stipend benchmarks, draft Japanese-language collateral, and handle career-office introductions properly.
4. **Pilot with Nagoya University G30**: Run the first cohort (2–3 interns) from G30 since their English ability is pre-validated by the program. Use success data as evidence for scaling to Science Tokyo and Waseda in year two.
5. **Set up a Tokyo-or-Osaka satellite contact** (even a single recruiter on the ground or a contracted agent) before the recruitment-cycle launch. Japanese career offices are highly relationship-driven; remote-only outreach from India will underperform.
6. **Attend on-campus events**: Specifically the **Nagoya University G30 Career Forum**, **Waseda's Summer Internship & Industry Research Fair** (May), and Science Tokyo's industry-collaboration symposia. Physical presence at one or two of these signals seriousness.
7. **Define a Japan-FTE pathway** (even a small one — 2–3 FTE slots/year in a Tokyo or Bangalore-hybrid model) before extending offers. Without it, conversion will collapse. Pair this with explicit visa-sponsorship commitments documented in the offer template.
