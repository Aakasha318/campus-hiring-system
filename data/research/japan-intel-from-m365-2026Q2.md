## 3. Celebal's existing university partnership template — the COE program

This is the most important finding. Celebal does not need to invent a new framework for Japan — it already operates a proven one. The **Centre of Excellence (COE)** program is the template the Japan engagement should adapt.

### COE Model
- **Pattern:** Train → Hire → Deploy
- **Duration:** 3–4 months
- **Vehicle:** MoU signed with institute → CT CoE lab setup → branded co-program → FDP + student engagement → hiring pipeline

### COE components / value proposition to universities
- Integrated Certification Program (COE)
- Faculty Development Program (FDP)
- Internship Program
- Early eligibility for Campus Hiring (market scenario dependent)
- Tech labs, research collaborations, hackathons, workshops
- Innovation hub on campus premises

### COE domains offered
App Modernization & iPaaS · Big Data, ETL & Analytics · Business Intelligence · Serverless Computing · Web & Cross-Platform App Development · DevOps · Cloud Infra & Automation · Cloud Security & Identity · Mixed Reality · ML & Computer Vision · CRMs and ERPs · Chatbot

### Currently active COE partnerships
Manipal University · JIET · Poornima Group of Institutions · DY Patil · Sanjivani Group of Colleges · Aarya College · ICFAI · LNMIIT · WIT · MMDU · SOA · Galgotias University · Lloyd Business School · Global Institute of Technology · KIIT · Galgotias College · ISIM · PCCOE · BC Roy College

### Implication for Japan
The Japan engagement should be positioned internally as **"COE Japan — Nagoya Pilot"**, not as a new framework. This:
- Reuses an executive-approved template (no new pitch to leadership needed)
- Reuses existing coordinator muscle (Priya / Shaifali team operates similar engagements today)
- Carries proven KPIs and reporting cadence into Japan
- Differentiates from Indian COEs only on: language (English-required), visa/FTE pathway, off-cycle timing, partnership term length (5-year MoU vs 3-year for Indian colleges)
