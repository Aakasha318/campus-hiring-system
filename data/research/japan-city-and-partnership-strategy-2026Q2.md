# Japan Campus Strategy — Decision Document

This document supersedes the comparative analysis in the companion files for the purpose of leadership decision-making. The shortlist and outreach pack remain valid as supporting material; this document tells you **where to invest, what to build, and what to commit to**.

---

## Decision

**Anchor the Japan campus program in Nagoya, with Nagoya University as the sole strategic partner for the first 12–18 months.** Open Tokyo (Science Tokyo, then Waseda) as a recruiting market in year 2 — but do not attempt to replicate the partnership depth there. Park Osaka entirely until a concrete business reason emerges.

## Why Nagoya (city-level rationale)

| Dimension | Tokyo | Osaka | **Nagoya** |
|---|---|---|---|
| English-medium undergraduate engineering | Graduate-level only (UTokyo GSC, Waseda FSE English tracks) | Contracting (Osaka U Mechanical English program discontinued 2024) | **G30 — undergrad engineering in English, unique in Japan** |
| Named POC accessible to foreign employers | Generic office-level only | Generic office-level only | **Michelle Kuhn, Graduate School of Informatics** |
| Gaishi-kei competition for talent | Brutal — Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, Mercari, Indeed | Moderate | **Low — Toyota-orbit dominates Japanese hiring; foreign tech sparse** |
| Shinsotsu collision | High (April mass-hiring pressure) | High | **Low — G30 cycles admit in October** |
| Cost of living | Highest | Mid | ~25–30% below Tokyo |
| Differentiation potential | Celebal is one of thousands | Limited industry-context match | **High — India-tech partner in a DX-hungry manufacturing hub** |
| Institutional appetite for international MoUs | Mixed (selective at top schools) | Lower (newer/smaller) | **High — Nagoya recently opened global campus at Edinburgh** |
| Tech-sharing relevance | Generic | Kansai industrial focus | **Direct match — Toyota/Denso/Aisin DX needs align with Celebal's data/cloud/AI** |

### The single biggest reason

Tokyo treats foreign tech firms as commodities. Nagoya treats them as partners. For collaboration that includes **technology sharing AND hiring**, city culture matters more than university rankings. Nagoya's current industrial identity is "we need digital transformation help; who can be our long-term partner?" — that is precisely Celebal's lane.

### What about Osaka?

Park entirely. Osaka offers nothing the other two cities don't, unless Celebal has existing Kansai delivery clients to anchor the relationship. Revisit only if (a) a business reason emerges, (b) regional government incentive program opens, or (c) acquisition/JV target materializes in Kansai.

## The Model — University Partnership, not Recruitment Program

The user's framing — *"collaboration which goes into technologies sharing, hiring prospective"* — points to a Master Partnership Agreement (MoU), not a recruiting calendar.

### Vehicle

**Master Partnership Agreement (MoU)** signed with Nagoya University's **Industry-Academia-Government Collaboration HQ** (`sangaku@t.mail.nagoya-u.ac.jp`, +81-52-747-6783).

- Term: 3–5 years
- Renewable annually after the initial period
- Signed at MD level on the Celebal side; faculty dean level on the Nagoya side

### Four work streams under the MoU

| # | Work stream | Celebal owner | Nagoya counterpart | Annual investment (Celebal) | Celebal's outcome | Nagoya's outcome |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Talent pipeline** — internships → FTE conversion | HR (Country Head sponsor) | Graduate School of Informatics + Career Support Center | ¥6–10M (stipends, relocation, recruitment ops) | 3–5 interns/year, ≥60% FTE conversion | Premium employer presence; documented placements for international students |
| 2 | **Joint capstone / sponsored research** | VP Engineering | Faculty PIs in Informatics, Engineering | ¥4–6M (project sponsorship + IP coverage) | IP-cleared insights on real industry problems; first look at top thesis projects | Industry-grade datasets and real problems for student/PhD work; co-publications |
| 3 | **Faculty engagement** — guest lectures, workshops, hackathons | Senior engineers (rotation) | G30 office + Informatics curriculum chair | Time, not cash (~3 engineer-weeks/year + travel) | Organic employer brand inside the student community | Industry-grade curriculum content; live case studies |
| 4 | **Bilateral exchange** — the "technology sharing" component | Joint (HR + Engineering) | International office + select student cohort | ¥3–5M (travel + housing both directions) | Selected students → 2-week immersion at Celebal Jaipur; Celebal engineers → 2-week residency at Nagoya labs | Cross-cultural global experience for students; ongoing research collaboration foundation |

**Total annual Celebal commitment:** ¥15–25M (~$100K–170K USD) — modest at enterprise scale. Reviewable annually against KPIs.

### Governance

- **Joint Steering Committee** — one Celebal senior (India Country Head or VP Engineering) + one Nagoya senior (Dean of Informatics or G30 Director)
- Quarterly cadence
- Annual MoU review with optional renewal/expansion
- Operational working group meets monthly (Celebal program owner + Nagoya admin lead)

### KPIs

| Metric | Year 1 target | Year 2 target | Year 3 target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interns hired from Nagoya | 3 | 5 | 8 |
| FTE conversion rate | 60% | 65% | 70% |
| 90-day retention | 85% | 90% | 90% |

| Mutual satisfaction (both sides, 1–5) | ≥4.0 | ≥4.2 | ≥4.5 |

## Phasing — Three-Year Arc

### Year 1 (FY2026–27): Nagoya only

- Sign MoU
- Pilot cohort of 3 interns from G30 + Informatics
- Sponsor one capstone project
- Deliver 2 guest lectures
- Send 1 Celebal engineer for 2-week residency at Nagoya
- Host 1 Nagoya student for 2-week immersion at Jaipur
- **Do not open Tokyo or Osaka.** Concentrate force.

### Year 2 (FY2027–28): Add Tokyo (Science Tokyo IGES) as a recruiting market

- Science Tokyo via `career.info@ssc.isct.ac.jp` — recruiting model only
- Use Nagoya pattern as recruiting playbook
- **Do not attempt to replicate the partnership depth in Tokyo** — city culture won't support it
- Deepen Nagoya: cohort to 5, second research project, joint publication target

### Year 3 (FY2028–29): Add Waseda for Tokyo volume

- Waseda Career Center + `intl-fse@list.waseda.jp`
- Tokyo remains recruiting-only
- Nagoya cohort to 8; explore possibility of named joint lab or Celebal Chair sponsorship

### Year 4+: Reconsider Osaka only on specific trigger

- Delivery client in Kansai
- Acquisition or JV target
- Regional government incentive program

## What Celebal must commit to BEFORE the first conversation with Nagoya

This section is a gate, not a checklist. **If any of these six items cannot be locked internally, do not send the *irai-jō*. A failed first overture damages credibility for years.**

1. **Japan-FTE pathway must exist.** Even 2–3 Japan-based or Japan-hybrid FTE slots per year. Not all-India relocation. Japanese students will not convert to a "move to Jaipur" offer; the entire program collapses without this commitment.
2. **Visa sponsorship capability must be confirmed.** Either Celebal Japan entity is established, or a contracted sponsor is identified and engaged. Specific to *Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services* visa category.
3. **MD-level signatory authority** for the MoU is pre-cleared with the Celebal board.
4. **Multi-year budget approved.** Minimum ¥15M/year × 3 years. Year-1-only investment is wasted; partnership value compounds.
5. **Single named program owner at Celebal.** One person, full ownership, sufficient seniority to make decisions. Not a committee. If this person cannot be named today, the program is not ready.
6. **Quarterly Celebal travel to Nagoya.** Someone senior physically present at least quarterly. Japan-style relationships die without face time.

## Why this is the right answer

The earlier shortlist and outreach pack answered "which universities and how to contact them." This document answers what was actually being asked: **where should Celebal invest its Japan strategy, and what should that strategy look like.**

The recommendation is **deliberately narrow**:

- One city (Nagoya), not three
- One partner (Nagoya University), not eight institutions
- One MoU, not a calendar of outreach
- One named Celebal owner, not a recruiting committee
- 12–18 months of focused execution before expanding

Concentration of force is the only way a foreign mid-cap consulting firm wins relationship-driven access in Japan. Trying to do Tokyo, Osaka, and Nagoya simultaneously will produce three half-relationships and no real partner. Doing Nagoya properly produces one anchor partnership that compounds.

## Open questions for Celebal leadership

Answer these before any commitment is made:

1. Is there an existing or planned **Celebal Japan entity**? If not, what is the timeline to establish one or partner with a Japan-based EOR (Employer of Record)?
2. What is the **proposed FTE distribution model** for Nagoya graduates — Japan-based, Japan-hybrid, India-based with frequent Japan travel?
3. What is the **annual budget envelope** the leadership team is comfortable approving for this partnership for FY26 / FY27 / FY28?
4. Who is the **named program owner** at Celebal? (Name, title, percentage of time committed.)
5. Are there any **existing relationships in Japan** — clients, partners, alumni — that should be leveraged for the introduction to Nagoya University?
6. What is the **MoU approval pathway** internally — board approval, legal review timeline, signature authority?
7. What is the **risk appetite** if Year 1 underperforms — pause, scale down, or cut entirely?
