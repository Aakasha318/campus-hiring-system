## 1. Recommended sequence — Nagoya → Science Tokyo → Waseda

All three top-3 universities tied at fit-score 9. The tiebreaker is **execution risk for the first cohort**.

### Pick: Start with Nagoya University

| Reason | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Only named human contact in entire shortlist** | Michelle Kuhn, International Student Advisor at Graduate School of Informatics — `kuhnm@i.nagoya-u.ac.jp`, +81-52-788-6269 |

| **Lower competitive intensity than Tokyo** | Toyota-ecosystem dominates locally; Celebal can carve a global-tech niche |

### Cycle 2: Add Institute of Science Tokyo

Trigger: Nagoya pilot yields < 2 viable candidates, OR program approved for scale.

Why second: Higher technical bar (IGES graduate cohort, densest CS in Japan), but more bureaucratic entry — Japanese-language follow-up expected via the `career-tasu` system. Post-2024 merger administrative churn adds risk.

### Cycle 3: Add Waseda

Trigger: headcount > 5/year, or need for volume in Tokyo.

Why third: Largest international student population in Japan, but Waseda does not publish a distinct international-student career sub-track contact. Most generic of the three first-contact channels.

## 3. Bilingual *irai-jō* (依頼状) — Formal Introduction Letter Template

> ⚠️ **The Japanese draft below is competent but not native-reviewed.** Have a Japanese-speaking colleague or your recruiting partner (Robert Walters Japan / Daijob / similar) review before sending. Japanese business letters carry high formality expectations and small phrasing errors damage credibility.

### Placeholders to fill in

| Placeholder | What to put |
|---|---|
| `[DATE]` | Letter date in both Japanese era and Gregorian (e.g., 令和8年5月26日 / May 26, 2026) |
| `[UNIVERSITY OFFICE NAME]` | Office name + 御中 (e.g., 名古屋大学 大学院情報学研究科 御中) |
| `[ATTN]` | Named recipient if known + 様 (e.g., Michelle Kuhn 様) |
| `[SIGNATORY_NAME]` | Senior Celebal signatory (MD, India Country Head, or VP HR) |
| `[SIGNATORY_TITLE]` | Title in English + Japanese (e.g., Managing Director / マネージング・ディレクター) |
| `[CELEBAL_JAPAN_ENTITY]` | Japan-entity name if it exists; else "via partner sponsorship" |
| `[STIPEND_AMOUNT]` | Monthly stipend in JPY (recommended floor: ¥200,000/month) |
| `[PILOT_HEADCOUNT]` | Target intern count for this university in pilot cycle (e.g., 2–3) |
| `[DATE_WINDOW]` | Window of availability for the requested meeting (e.g., "any weekday between 10:00–17:00 JST during June 2026") |
| `[SPECIFIC_REASON]` | Per-university customization — see table at end of section |

## 4. Pre-send checklist

Before sending the *irai-jō* to any university, confirm internally:

- [ ] **Japan-FTE pathway commitment** is real (even 2–3 slots/year). Without this, the offer is hollow and conversion rates collapse.
- [ ] **Visa sponsorship capability** is confirmed (Japan entity or contracted sponsor). Do not promise what cannot be delivered.
- [ ] **Stipend amount** benchmarked against gaishi-kei norms (¥150K–300K/month for engineering long-term internships; floor: ¥200K to be competitive).
- [ ] **Signatory** is sufficiently senior (MD, Country Head, or VP+). Junior signatories signal lack of commitment.
- [ ] **Japanese portion** native-reviewed (Robert Walters JP / Daijob / native colleague).
- [ ] **Company overview** PDF attachment prepared in both English and Japanese.
- [ ] **Reply tracking** set up in shared Outlook mailbox or SharePoint `OutreachLog` list — every reply needs an owner.
- [ ] **Timing**: Send during **September–November 2026** for 2027 intake recruiting cycle. Sending outside this window risks the message being deprioritized.
