## Recommendation

**Anchor the Japan engagement in Nagoya University, structured as a COE Japan Pilot — 5-year MoU, FY26–27 launch, target 3 interns in Year 1.** Defer Tokyo and Osaka to subsequent years.

The reasoning, in one paragraph: Nagoya University is the only Japanese university operating English-medium *undergraduate* engineering at scale (the MEXT-designated G30 program). It admits in October — meaning a 6-month internship does not collide with Japan's April mass-hiring (shinsotsu) cycle that locks out students elsewhere. We also have a named human contact there (Michelle Kuhn, International Student Advisor, Graduate School of Informatics) — every other shortlisted university returns only generic office-level contacts. And Nagoya is the heart of Japan's manufacturing-tech digital transformation (Toyota, Denso, Aisin) — a regional context where Celebal's data/cloud/AI positioning lands directly, unlike Tokyo where we'd be one of thousands of foreign tech firms.

## What this looks like operationally

I propose adapting our existing **COE model** (currently active with Manipal, Poornima, DY Patil, Sanjivani, KIIT, SOA, and others) for Japan. Same four pillars — talent pipeline, sponsored capstone research, faculty engagement, bilateral exchange — with Japan-specific adaptations: English-required, October cycle, Japan-FTE pathway commitment, and a 5-year MoU term rather than the 3-year used domestically.

The four-stream investment envelope works out to roughly ¥15–25M/year (approx ₹85L–1.4Cr). Reviewable annually against KPIs. I can share the detailed allocation if useful.

## What I need from you (and Anupam) to move forward

Six commitments before sending the first formal outreach (依頼状 *irai-jō*) to Nagoya. If any of these can't be locked, I recommend holding the outreach — a failed first overture in Japan damages credibility for years:

1. **Japan-FTE pathway** — 2–3 Japan-based or Japan-hybrid FTE slots/year. Japanese students will not convert to "relocate to Jaipur" offers; the pilot collapses without this.
2. **Visa sponsorship** — Celebal Japan entity or contracted EOR sponsor for Engineer/Specialist visa.
3. **MoU signatory authority** at MD level, pre-cleared.
4. **Multi-year budget commitment** — minimum ¥15M/year for 3 years.
5. **Quarterly travel cadence** — someone senior on the ground in Nagoya quarterly (I can be that person if the role is approved).
6. **Confirmation of Q3 FY27 (Sep–Nov 2026) as the outreach window** — aligned with Japanese internship recruiting cycle for 2027 intake.
