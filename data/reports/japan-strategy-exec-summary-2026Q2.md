# Japan Campus Strategy — Executive Summary (v2)

## The recommendation in one line

**Launch a CFT Japan pilot in Nagoya, structured as a COE-model partnership with Nagoya University. 5-year MoU. FY26–27 launch. 3 interns in Year 1.**

## Why this framing

The Japan engagement is not a new framework. It is a geographic extension of two programs Celebal already runs:

1. **CFT (Celebal Frontier Talent)** — our 2027-batch umbrella program for Tier-1 / Edge-Tier-1 engineering campuses. Currently India-only. Japan becomes the first international extension.
2. **COE (Centre of Excellence)** — our institutional partnership model (active with Manipal, JIET, Poornima, DY Patil, Sanjivani, KIIT, SOA, Galgotias, Lloyd, BC Roy, PCCOE, and ~13 others). Train → Hire → Deploy. MoU-driven. Japan adapts this template; we do not invent a new one.

Externally, students see **CFT Japan**. Institutionally, Nagoya University signs into **COE Japan**. Same delivery muscle (Priya, Shaifali, Prerna, Priyanshi, Sharthak) extends to Japan with Akash leading.

## Why Nagoya, not Tokyo or Osaka

Three structural advantages no other Japanese city offers:

1. **G30 = English-medium undergraduate engineering** (unique in Japan). Language readiness is pre-validated by the program itself; no language gamble at selection.
2. **Only named human POC in entire 8-university shortlist** — Michelle Kuhn, International Student Advisor, Graduate School of Informatics. Every other university returns generic office-level contacts. Japanese partnerships are built person-to-person; Nagoya is the only one where that's already possible.
3. **Off-cycle from shinsotsu** — G30 admits in October. A 6-month internship does not collide with Japan's April mass-hiring window, which structurally locks out off-cycle programs elsewhere.

Tokyo is over-courted by FAANG-tier firms; we'd be one of thousands of foreign tech companies. Osaka offers no advantages the others don't. **One city, one university, one MoU** beats trying three simultaneously.

## What the partnership looks like (adapted COE)

Four parallel work streams under a Master Partnership Agreement (5-year term, vs 3-year used domestically):

| Stream | What we do | What we get | Annual investment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Talent pipeline | 3–5 CFT interns → FTE conversion | Pre-vetted developer talent | ¥6–10M |
| Sponsored capstones | Fund 1–3 student projects on real Celebal problems | IP-cleared insights; first look at top thesis work | ¥4–6M |
| Faculty engagement | Guest lectures, workshops, hackathons | Employer brand inside the student community | Time, not cash |
| Bilateral exchange | Jaipur ↔ Nagoya residencies (technology sharing) | Cross-cultural transfer in both directions | ¥3–5M |

**Total annual commitment: ¥15–25M (~₹85L–1.4Cr)**, reviewable annually against CFT-inherited KPIs.

## KPIs (inherit from CFT 2027 + Japan-specific)

| Metric | CFT 2027 target | Japan pilot target |
|---|---|---|
| Offer-to-joining ratio | ≥ 85% | ≥ 85% |
| Candidate NPS | ≥ 60 | ≥ 60 |
| Compliance breaches | Zero | Zero |
| Interns hired from Nagoya | n/a | 3 (Year 1) → 5 (Y2) → 8 (Y3) |
| 90-day retention | n/a | ≥ 85% |
| FTE conversion rate | n/a | ≥ 60% |
| Joint research projects | n/a | 1 → 2 → 3 |
| Mutual satisfaction (1–5) | n/a | ≥ 4.0 |

## Six commitments we need from leadership before sending the *irai-jō*

If any of these can't be locked, the program isn't ready and the first overture should not be sent. **A failed first overture in Japan damages credibility for years.**

1. **Japan-FTE pathway** — 2–3 Japan-based or Japan-hybrid FTE slots per year. Japanese students do not convert to "relocate to Jaipur" offers; conversion collapses without this.
2. **Visa sponsorship capability** — Celebal Japan entity OR contracted EOR sponsor for *Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services* visa.
3. **MoU signatory authority** at MD level, pre-cleared. Use the COE MoU template (Day 5 / Track B of Hiring Readiness 2027 doc).
4. **Multi-year budget** — minimum ¥15M/year × 3 years.
5. **Quarterly travel cadence** — Akash on the ground in Nagoya at least quarterly.
6. **Outreach window: September–November 2026** — aligned with Japanese internship recruiting cycle for 2027 intake.
