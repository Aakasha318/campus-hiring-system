# Japan Campus Strategy — Executive Summary

## The recommendation in one line

**Sign a 5-year strategic partnership MoU with Nagoya University in FY2026–27. Don't open Tokyo or Osaka until Year 2.**

## Why Nagoya, not Tokyo or Osaka

Three reasons that don't appear in any ranking table:

1. **The only university in Japan with English-medium undergraduate engineering** (G30 program). Every other candidate forces us to wait for graduate students or accept Japanese-language candidates and screen for English. Nagoya solves the language problem at source.
2. **The only university with a named human POC we can write to today** (Michelle Kuhn, Graduate School of Informatics, `kuhnm@i.nagoya-u.ac.jp`). Every other shortlisted university returns generic office-level contacts. Long-term partnerships are built person-to-person; Nagoya is the only one where that's already possible.
3. **Nagoya is actively seeking digital transformation partners.** It's the heart of Japan's manufacturing-tech transformation (Toyota, Denso, Aisin DX). In Tokyo, Celebal is one of thousands of foreign tech firms. In Nagoya, Celebal can be the India-tech partner in a city looking for one.

Tokyo is over-courted by FAANG-tier firms and treats foreign companies as commodities. Osaka offers no advantages the other cities don't. **One city, one university, one MoU** beats trying to do three cities at once.

## What the partnership looks like

A Master Partnership Agreement (3–5 year term) with four parallel work streams:

| Work stream | What we do | What we get | What it costs Celebal/year |
|---|---|---|---|
| Talent pipeline | 3–5 interns/year → FTE conversion | Pre-vetted developer talent | ¥6–10M |
| Sponsored research / capstones | Fund 1–3 student projects on real Celebal problems | IP-cleared insights; first look at top thesis work | ¥4–6M |
| Faculty engagement | Guest lectures, workshops, hackathons | Employer brand inside the student community | Time, not cash |
| Bilateral exchange | Students visit Jaipur; engineers visit Nagoya | Technology sharing in both directions | ¥3–5M |

**Total annual commitment: ¥15–25M (~$100K–170K USD)**, reviewable annually against KPIs.

## Year 1 KPIs (the gate for Year 2 expansion)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Interns hired from Nagoya | 3 |
| FTE conversion rate | 60% |
| 90-day retention | 85% |
| Joint research projects completed | 1 |
| Student events delivered | 4 |
| Mutual satisfaction (both sides, 1–5) | ≥4.0 |

## Expansion path

- **Year 2:** Add Institute of Science Tokyo (recruiting-only, not partnership) → +3–5 interns/year
- **Year 3:** Add Waseda University (recruiting-only) for Tokyo volume → +3–5 interns/year
- **Year 4+:** Osaka only on specific business trigger

## Six commitments we need from Celebal leadership before sending the first letter

If any of these can't be locked, the program isn't ready and the first overture should not be sent:

1. **Japan-FTE pathway.** 2–3 Japan-based or Japan-hybrid FTE slots per year. Japanese students will not convert to "move to Jaipur" offers; conversion collapses without this.
2. **Visa sponsorship capability.** Celebal Japan entity OR a contracted Employer of Record sponsor, supporting *Engineer/Specialist in Humanities/International Services* visa.
3. **MD-level signatory authority** pre-cleared for the MoU.
4. **Multi-year budget** approved at minimum ¥15M/year × 3 years. Year-1-only spend produces no return.
5. **Single named program owner at Celebal.** One person, sufficient seniority, willing to commit 30%+ time. If we can't name them today, the program is not ready.
6. **Quarterly travel cadence.** Someone senior physically in Nagoya at least every 3 months. Japan-style relationships die without face time.

## What we need leadership to decide this quarter

1. **Approve or reject Nagoya as the anchor partner.**
2. **Approve the 3-year budget envelope** (or counter-propose).
3. **Name the program owner.**
4. **Confirm Japan-FTE distribution model** (Japan-based / hybrid / India-based with travel).
5. **Authorize sending the first outreach letter** in the September 2026 window.

## Risk if we do nothing

The September–November 2026 window is the standard Japanese internship recruitment kickoff for the 2027 intake. Missing it pushes the program 12 months. Competitors targeting the same universities (Indian IT majors expanding Japan offices) will be active in this window.
